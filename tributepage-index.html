Salahuddin Yusuf Ibn Ayyub

The Victorious King, Righteousness of the World and the Faith, Yusuf ibn Ayyub

The Salahuddin Timeline
  • 1137: Born as Yusuf ibn Ayyub in Tikrit, Upper Mesopotamia (modern-day Iraq)
  • 1164: Joined his uncle Shirkuh on a military campaign to Egypt
  • 1169: Became the vizier of the Fatimid Caliphate in Egypt
  • 1171: Abolished the Fatimid Caliphate and aligned Egypt with the Sunni Abbasid Caliphate
  • 1174: Became the Sultan of Egypt and Syria
  • 1174-1176: Conquest of Yemen. Salahuddin expanded his influence beyond the Levant by conquering Yemen. This campaign helped secure his control over the Red Sea trade routes and increased his resources.
  • 1177: Battle of Montgisard. Salahuddin faced a significant defeat at the hands of the Crusaders led by King Baldwin IV of Jerusalem. Despite having a larger army, Salahuddin's forces were overwhelmed by the Crusaders' strategic maneuvers.
  • 1183: Siege of Kerak. Salahuddin laid siege to the fortress of Kerak, held by the Crusaders. The siege was part of his broader strategy to weaken Crusader strongholds in the region. He eventually lifted the siege due to the arrival of reinforcements led by the King of Jerusalem, Guy of Lusignan.
  • 1187: Battle of Hattin. This was a decisive victory for Salahuddin, leading to the capture of Jerusalem. The battle demonstrated his military genius and ability to unite Muslim forces against the Crusaders.
  • 1189-1192: Third Crusade. Salahuddin faced Richard the Lionheart and other European leaders in a series of battles and sieges. Despite the intense fighting, the Crusaders failed to recapture Jerusalem, and a truce was eventually signed in 1192, allowing Christian pilgrims access to the holy city.
  • 1193: Passed away in Damascus, Syria

Salahuddin Ayyubi's campaigns were marked by his strategic brilliance, military prowess, and ability to inspire loyalty among his troops. His legacy continues to be celebrated for his contributions to the Muslim world and his role in the Crusades.

"Do not underestimate the power of kindness."
